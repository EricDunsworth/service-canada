Our commitment to improving passport processing

Service Canada is committed to improving the current passport processing delays. We understand that the current wait times are unacceptable and Canadians expect more from their government.

Service Canada will keep working hard to reduce wait times for passports, and to improve delivery of all the services Canadians count on from us.

Our solutions

So far, the Government has:

  • established a simplified renewal process for any adult passport issued in the last 15 years
  • launched an appointment booking tool that directs clients to the right location for service - clients should check back often, as appointments are added as they become available
  • added processing hubs across the country
  • increased in-person service capacity
  • begun to publish approximate passport wait times to help clients prepare for their visit at a passport office, and these numbers are updated 3 times a day
  • implemented a triage strategy in select metropolitan areas and extended it in other large cities as required

Workforce

The passport workforce has increased by over 900 employees since July 2021. Clerical staff to support intake and administrative tasks have also increased. In addition, data entry functions have expanded across the existing workforce of over 1200 staff in more than 300 Service Canada Centres. This allows passport officers to focus on entitling (approving) passports. Training on renewals and simple applications has also expanded across the existing workforce.

The workforce is distributed across the country and are working on the national inventory to ensure consistency across the country.
