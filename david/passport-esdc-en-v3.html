Passport services

  1. When are you travelling

    The process to follow depends on when you need the passport for.

    • Within the next 2 business days (urgent requests)

      Bring the completed application form to a specialized passport site that offers urgent pick-up service. A proof of travel or need is required.

      Do not book an appointment.

    • Within the next 25 business days

      Bring the completed application form to a specialized passport site. A proof of travel or need is required.

      Book an appointment or go to a specialized passport site - Appointments are recommended as walk-in service is offered only if capacity allows.

    • Beyond the next 25 business days

      You can apply by mail or in person at your local Service Canada Centre. No proof of travel or need is required.

      Book your appointment online at eServiceCanada - Appointments are recommended as walk-in service is offered only if capacity allows.

  2. Find the right form and documents for your situation

    • Renewing your passport (if lost, stolen, damaged or expired)

      You can renew your passport if:

      • you were 16 or older on the date you applied
      • your passport was valid for 5 to 10 years
      • your passport was issued within 15 years of the expiry date on it, and
      • your new passport will have the same name, date of birth, place of birth and gender identifier as your old passport
      How to renew your passport
    • New adult passport application

      To apply for a Canadian passport, you must be:

      • a Canadian citizen, and
      • 16 years of age or older
      How to apply for a passport
    • Child’s passport (under the age of 16)

      You can only apply for a child’s passport if:

      • one of the child’s parents (if you’re not separated or divorced)
      • the parent who has custody of the child (if you are separated or divorced) with the correct documentation
      • the child’s legal guardian, with the correct documentation
      How to apply for a child passport

    Application fees

    You can pay your application fees by:

    • certified cheque or money order (postal or bank) made payable to the Receiver General for Canada
    • credit card (Visa, MasterCard, American Express)
    • debit card

  4. Check the status of your application

    Generally, processing time at specialized passport sites requires up to 10 business days, plus mailing time. Some offices offer urgent and/or express and pick-up service.

    If you apply at a regular Service Canada office, processing time can be up to 20 business days, plus mailing time.

    Longer than usual processing time

    We are currently experiencing a very high volume of requests. Your application may take longer than the regular processing times.
