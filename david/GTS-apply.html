Before you apply

Review this checklist:

If you decide to apply under a different stream after you have submitted an application, you must cancel your original application and reapply under the new stream. New processing fees will apply.

Employers applying to the Global Talent Stream (GTS) can expect:

  • LMIA processing time of 10 business days (80% of the time) beginning the first business day following the day Service Canada receives the application
  • support throughout the application process by GTS Officers
  • accelerated work permit processing by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) for approved applicants (10 business day, 80% of the time)
  • For positions located in Quebec:
    • Applications will need to be submitted to Service Canada and the Quebec’s ministère de l’immigration, de la Francisation, et l’Intégration (MIFI) at the same time
    • MIFI will only accept applications submitted in French

How to apply

The fastest way to apply is to apply online.

Apply online

LMIA Online is a reliable and secure platform allowing you to complete and submit an online application to Service Canada.

To access LMIA Online you must have a Job Bank account.

Create a Job Bank account

  • To create a Job Bank for employers account
  • It takes approximately 10 minutes to register
  • Once your Job Bank account(s) is/are created, you will be able to submit an LMIA application via the LMIA Online Portal

Access LMIA Online

  • Log in through Job Bank for employers to access the LMIA Online Portal
  • Your account details will be required for authentication purposes

Submit your application

  • Create and fill out your LMIA application
  • Upload all required documents supporting your business legitimacy (if applicable)
  • Submit the application form along with the required documents, and the processing fee via the LMIA Online Portal

Alternative: Apply by completing the application form

If you can't use LMIA Online, complete the GTS Application Form (EMP5624)

  • Make sure you use the latest version of the application form
  • Ensure all fields are completed

If you are applying for multiple positions at the same time, you can complete and submit the GTS – Annex 1 Additional Occupation form (EMP5625) instead of completing EMP5624 again.

  • Include all required documents to support your business legitimacy (if applicable)

If you submit an application for work in Quebec, and the work period is more than 30 days, you must send a printed copy of your LMIA application to MIFI after submission at the same time as you submit to Service Canada.

For more information about positions located in Quebec, please consult Quebec’s MIFI website (French only).

Processing fee

There is a $1,000 fee for each position requested under the GTS. These fees cover the cost of processing your application.

The processing fee payment (in Canadian dollars) can be made by:

  • Visa
  • MasterCard
  • American Express
  • Certified cheque (shall be made payable to the Receiver General for Canada)
  • Money Order (shall be made payable to the Receiver General for Canada)
  • Bank draft (shall be made payable to the Receiver General for Canada)

If the type of credit card is not listed, then it is not accepted by Service Canada.

The processing fee cannot be paid by nor be recovered from the TFWs.

You will not receive a process fee refund if you:

  • withdraw your application
  • receive a negative assessment; or,
  • choose another TFW program stream

Refunds are issued only if a fee was collected in error.

Using a third-party

If you use the services of a third-party representative (paid or unpaid), you must complete the third-party information sections of the application.

You must not recover the costs for these services from the TFW.

Service Canada may communicate with you directly to check the information on the application form from the third-party representative. Third party representatives cannot negotiate the LMBP on your behalf.

Service Canada will not mediate a dispute between you and a third-party representative. We will not communicate complaints to a regulatory body on your behalf.

If you have a complaint about your third-party representative, there are ways to get help.

Paid third-party representatives

You can only pay a representative to help you with your application or fill it out for you, if they are authorized.

An authorized third-party representative is a:

  • member in good standing of a Canadian provincial or territorial law society or students-at-law under their supervision, or the Chambre des notaires du Québec
  • paralegal in the Province of Ontario's law society
  • member in good standing of the Immigration Consultants of Canada Regulatory Council

Unpaid third-party representatives

Someone else can still help you, even if they are not authorized to receive payment. An unpaid representative can be a:

  • family member
  • friend
  • not-for-profit group
  • religious organization
