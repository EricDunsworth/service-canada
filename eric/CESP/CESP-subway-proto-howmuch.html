Canada Learning Bond amounts and eligibility

If you're eligible to receive Canada Learning Bond (the Bond), you could receive $500 the first year you're eligible, then another $100 every year after that, up to a lifetime maximum of $2,000.

Bond eligibility

You do not need to contribute money to your Registered Education Savings Plan (RESP) to receive the Bond. Eligibility for the the Bond is based on the number of qualified children in the family, and the adjusted income of the

primary caregiver

Primary caregiver

To be eligible for the Bond, the child must:

  • be a resident of Canada
  • have a Social Insurance Number (SIN)
  • be named as a beneficiary in an RESP
  • be born on or after January 1, 2004
  • be from a low-income family
Adjusted family income definition

The adjusted family income is the amount used, in part, to determine eligibility for the Bond and the amount of the Grant. Adjusted family income is determined by your net income (line 236 of their income tax and benefit return):

  • plus that of your spouse or common-law partner, where applicable
  • minus any Universal Child Care Benefit (UCCB) and Registered Disability Savings Plan (RDSP) income received
  • plus any UCCB and RDSP amounts repaid

Grant amounts and eligibility

The Canada Education Savings Grant (the Grant) provides 20% of the RESP contributions up to $2,500 each year. That means the Grant can add a maximum of $500 to an RESP each year, but children from middle- and low-income families might be eligible to get more from the Grant each year.

You have to make contributions to your RESP to get the Grant. The maximum lifetime amount of the Grant a child can get is $7,200.

Grant carry forward

The child can get more than the yearly amount of the Grant each year, under certain circumstances. Unused Grant amounts from previous years accumulate until the end of the year in which the child turns 17, even if they are not a beneficiary of an RESP.

If there is an unused grant amount from previous years, the subscriber can contribute more than $2,500 to the RESP per year and receive up to 20% of their contributions (up to $5,000) each year. This way, a child could get up to $1,000 of the Grant in their RESP per calendar year if there are unused amounts from previous years.

As long as the lifetime Grant limit ($7,200) is not exceeded, the Grant room can be carried forward.

  • plus that of your spouse or common-law partner, where applicable

Grant eligibility

The Grant, including the amount of the Grant, is available until the end of the calendar year that a child turns 17. However, there are eligibility restrictions for children who are 16 or 17 years old. A contribution must be made to the RESP to receive the Grant.

To be eligible for the Grant, the child must:

  • be a resident of Canada
  • have a Social Insurance Number (SIN)
  • be named as a beneficiary in an RESP
  • be 17 years old or younger

Use the calendar year to determine:

  • Grant eligibility
  • the amount of contributions made
  • the Grant room earned and used in the last year
Grant eligibility for children aged 16 or 17

Children who are 16 or 17 years old may be eligible to get the Grant. To be eligible, they must meet at least one of the following conditions before the end of the calendar year they turn 15:

  • at least $2,000 is contributed to (and not withdrawn from) the RESP
  • a minimum annual contribution of $100 is made to (and not withdrawn from) the RESP in any four previous years

Extra benefits for people in Saskatchewan, British Columbia, and Quebec

Saskatchewan, British Columbia, and Quebec offer provincial grants that may add money to an RESP. This is on top of any money from the federal Bond and/or Grant.

Saskatchewan Advantage Grant for Education Savings (SAGES)

The Government of Saskatchewan will provide the Saskatchewan Advantage Grant for Education Savings (SAGES) of 10% on contributions made since January 1, 2013, to an RESP. The maximum grant is $250 per child per year. To be eligible for the grant, the child must be a resident of Saskatchewan when the contribution is made.

British Columbia Training and Education Savings Grant (BCTESG)

The B.C. Government will contribute $1,200 to eligible children through the B.C. Training and Education Savings Grant (BCTESG).

To be eligible for the $1,200 BCTESG:

  • Parent and child must be residents of B.C.
  • Child must have been born in 2006 or later
  • Child must be named as beneficiary of a RESP with a participating financial institution
Quebec Education Savings Incentive (QESI)

The Québec education savings incentive (QESI) is a tax measure that encourages Québec families to start saving early for the post-secondary education of their children and grandchildren.
