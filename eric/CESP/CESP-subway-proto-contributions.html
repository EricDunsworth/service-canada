RESP, Bond, and Grant lifetime limits

Some types of RESP require specific monthly contributions. Other types let you put money into your RESP whenever you want. Check with your RESP promoter before or after you open an RESP. You do not need to make contibutions to receive the Canada Learning Bond (the Bond).

RESP yearly and lifetime contribution limits

  • there is no annual limit to how much you can contribute to your RESP;
  • over the lifetime of the RESP, the maximum that can be contributed is $50,000.

Bond lifetime maximum

The lifetime maximum amount a beneficiary can receive from the Bond is $2,000

Grant lifetime maximum

The lifetime maximum amount a beneficiary can receive from the Grant is $7,200. Even if this limit has not been reached, no Grant amount will be given for any RESP contribution that exceeds the $50,000 lifetime RESP contribution limit.

To determine if the lifetime contribution limit has been exceeded, the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) takes into account all contributions made for the beneficiary, even if they have beenwithdrawn.

Over-contributions

An over-contribution occurs when the total contributions made to a beneficiary in one or more RESP(s) exceed the lifetime limit of $50,000.

When an over-contribution occurs, you will be required to pay tax in the amount of one percent per month on your share of the over-contribution until it is withdrawn.

Withdrawing contributions

Under normal circumstances, a withdrawal of contributions will require a repayment of the Grant, except when:

  • you withdraw contributions to eliminate an over-contribution; and
  • the total over-contribution is not more than $4,000 at the time of withdrawal; or
  • the RESP beneficiary is eligible for an Educational Assistance Payment (EAP).

If the total over-contribution is more than $4,000 at the time of withdrawal, the Grant is repayable on the entire amount withdrawn. If this happens, the grant room is not restored by the repaid amount of the Grant.

For more information on over-contributions as well as tax penalties, consult the CRA.

Tracking RESP contributions

It's important to track how much you contribute to your RESP, especially if you have more than one. Make sure you don't over-contribute. You should also track how much Grant you receive to make sure you don't go over the Grant lifetime limit.

Family plan contributions

If you have a family plan with two or more children, contributions must be tracked for each child named in the plan. You can make more than one contribution at a time, and the amounts do not have to be the same for each child.

Group plan contributions

When you open a group plan, you agree to make contributions into the group RESP at set times for the duration of the RESP contract.

The RESP promoter will credit the money you put into the group RESP to an account in your name. Any government Bond or grants received by the beneficiary will be put into a separate account that is in the beneficiary’s name.

The interest earned on your savings can be shared within the group plan; however, the interest earned on government grants or bonds cannot be shared and goes directly to the beneficiary named in the RESP.
