Calculation of your weekly benefit

You could get up to 55% of your earnings

For most people, the basic rate for calculating Employment Insurance (EI) benefits is 55% of their average insurable weekly earnings, up to a maximum amount.

Insurable earnings is income that is earned on which EI premiums were paid. It's used to calculate the weekly benefit rate to which you're entitled, including insurable tips and commissions.

As of January 1, 2022, the maximum yearly insurable earnings amount is $60,300. This means that you can receive a maximum amount of $638 per week.

How we calculate the amount

These are the steps we take to calculate your amount of weekly benefits:

  1. We determine the number of required best weeks, based on your regional rate of unemployment.
    Required number of weeks based on the unemployment rate

    In regions of Canada with the highest rates of unemployment, we’ll calculate using the best 14 weeks. In regions of Canada with the lowest rates of unemployment, we’ll use the best 22 weeks. In other regions, the number of weeks used to calculate benefits will be somewhere between 14 and 22, depending on the unemployment rate in those regions.

    Required number of best weeks by the regional rate of unemployment
    Regional rate of unemployment Required weeks
    6% or less 22
    6.1% to 7% 21
    7.1% to 8% 20
    8.1% to 9% 19
    9.1% to 10% 18
    10.1% to 11% 17
    11.1% to 12% 16
    12.1% to 13% 15
    13.1% or more 14

    To find out the rate of unemployment in your region, visit EI Program Characteristics.

    Once the weekly benefit rate is established, it will remain unchanged over the life of your claim.

  2. We calculate your total insurable earnings for the required number of best weeks (the weeks that you earned the most money) based on the information you provide and/or your record(s) of employment.
  3. We determine your average weekly earnings by dividing your total insurable earnings for your best weeks by your required number of best weeks.
  4. We then multiply the result by 55% to determine how much you will receive in weekly benefits.

Calculation

  • Total insurable earnings for the required number of best weeks
  • divided by Required number of best weeks
  • times by 55 %
  • equals Weekly EI benefit (maximum $638)

How long will your benefits last

You can receive EI from 14 weeks up to a maximum of 45 weeks.

The number of weeks of benefits depends on 2 factors:

  • the unemployment rate in your region at the time of filing your claim
    • use your postal code to search for your EI Economic Region
  • the amount of insurable hours you've accumulated in the last 52 weeks or since your last claim, whichever is shorter.

The number of weeks for which you may receive benefits doesn’t change if you move to another region after your benefit period begins.

Seasonal workers

If you’re a seasonal worker, you may be eligible for 5 additional weeks of benefits. The maximum number of weeks remains 45 weeks.

If your net family income is $25,921 or less

You may be eligible to receive the family supplement if:

  • your annual net family income is $25,921 or less
  • you have at least one child under 18
  • you or your spouse receive the Canada Child Benefit

We automatically add your family supplement to your weekly benefit payments. You don't need to take any action. Your total weekly amount can't exceed $638.

Taxes are deducted from EI payments

EI benefits are taxable, no matter what type of benefits you receive. Federal and provincial or territorial taxes, where applicable, will therefore be deducted from your payment.
