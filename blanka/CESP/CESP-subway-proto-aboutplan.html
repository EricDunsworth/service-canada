How the RESP, the Bond, and the grants work together

On this page

  • How an RESP works
  • The Bond and grants you could get
  • We send out letters to remind you

A Registered Education Savings Plan (RESP) is a special savings account to help save for a child's education after high school. An RESP allows adults to earn interest on their RESP tax-free.

When you open a plan, you may also be eligible to get the Bond, the Grant, and possibly provincial grants, from the Government of Canada to help with your long-term savings.

How an RESP works

Anyone can open an RESP account for a child — parents, guardians, grandparents, other relatives, and friends.

The subscriber is the person who opens the RESP with a promoter. The promoter is usually a financial institution. The beneficiary is the person who will get money from the RESP to pay for their education after high school — usually a child. Once a subscriber opens an RESP, they can make optional contributions, and apply for grants and bonds. The money in the RESP helps to pay the costs of a child's full- or part-time studies after high school at:

  • apprenticeship programs
  • CEGEPs
  • trade schools
  • colleges
  • universities
Detailed definitions for subscriber, promoter and beneficiary

Subscriber

Anyone can be a subscriber and open an RESP for a child. This includes parents, guardians, grandparents, other relatives, and friends. The subscriber can also choose to make contributions. Contributions to the RESP are needed to receive the Grant, but not the Bond.

Beneficiary

The beneficiary uses the RESP money for their education. The beneficiary can receive money from the RESP to pay for education after high school, as long as the school and program are eligible. The beneficiary can be a child or an adult. The beneficiary can be the same person as the subscriber, if an adult opens an RESP for themselves. There can be more than one beneficiary for each RESP.

Promoter

The promoter is the financial organization where you open the RESP. The promoter offers RESPs to the public. They administer all amounts contributed into the RESP and provide money from the RESP to the beneficiary when it is needed for eligible expenses for education after high school.

The Bond and grants you could get

When you have an RESP, you may be eligible for the Bond, and grants.

  • The Canada Learning Bond (the Bond) provides more money for low-income families. No contributions to the RESP are needed to receive the Bond.
  • The Canada Education Savings Grant (the Grant) provides 20% of contributions made to an RESP, up to a limit
  • RESP beneficiaries living in Saskatchewan, British Columbia, and Quebec may be eligible for additional benefits from their province.

We send out letters to remind you

The Government of Canada sends out letters to people who are eligible for benefits. If you received a letter from us and want to make sure it is legitimate, you can see our list of mailings here.
