Grants, contributions, and funding process typess

From: Employment and Social Development Canada

This page describes the difference between a grant and a contribution and the different funding processes used to select participants.

Grants and contributions

ESDC uses two different ways to issue funding for programs: Grants and Contributions. They are also referred to as transfer payments.


Grants: Option 1 of 2

Grants are payments that the government makes to individuals or organizations for activities that meet specific eligibility criteria. In most cases, there are no further conditions after the payment is received, however there can be exceptions depending on the program requirements. A recipient can also be required to report on results achieved.

About grants

Contributions: Option 2 of 2

Contributions are similar to grants however instead of a payment, an organisation can get reimbursed for specific costs if they meet eligibility criteria and specific conditions. As a recipient, you have to account for the funds you receive, and the Government can examine how the funds have been used throughout the duration of a project. You could also be subject to an audit.

About contributions

Hybrid model

In certain circumstances, when a program has multiple funding streams that have different objectives, a hybrid model is used and can include both grants and contributions.

Funding processes

To issue grants or contributions, ESDC programs uses different funding processes to select recipients.

  • Call for proposals

    A Call for Proposal is an open, formal, and planned process with a specific start and end date. It is used to identify eligible applicants interested in achieving program objectives and expected outcomes.

    Applicants submit a complete, costed application for funding consideration. The focus is on the eligibility and viability of the project activities and the eligibility and capacity of the applicant organization to manage the project appropriately according to the program Terms and Conditions.

  • Call for concepts

    A Call for Concept is a formal process used to identify eligible applicants interested in achieving program objectives and expected outcomes. Applicants submit a concept paper-based on the parameters of the Call for Concept. The focus is on the eligibility of the project “idea” rather than a fully detailed project proposal. Consideration is also given to the organisation as well. As a result of the assessment process, the Department may invite successful applicants to submit a full proposal.

  • Expression of interest

    An Expressing of Interest is a formal process where applicants express their interest in program funding to achieve the Department's policy priorities, program objectives and expected outcomes. The focus is on the eligibility of the organization (based on program-specific assessment criteria) rather than on a project idea. As a result of the screening process, the Department may invite successful EOI applicants to submit a concept paper or a proposal.

  • Continuous intake

    Continuous intake is a process where the Department funding is available to the public for specific program on a long-term basis. This process has no specific deadline for the submission of applications and applicants can apply for funding at any time. Assessment of applications is based on program-specific assessment criteria, objectives and expected results as well as budget availability.

  • Solicited applications

    A program may solicit specific organizations to submit an application for consideration following a Call for concepts or Expression of Interest or when an unanticipated situation arises requiring a rapid response. This process can be used when the Department has significant knowledge on the limited pool of organizations that could deliver expected results.
