Registered Disability Savings Plan

The Registered Disability Savings Plan (RDSP) is a long-term savings plan to help people with disabilities and their families. The plan beneficiary is the person with a severe and prolonged disability who will receive the money in the future.

When you open a plan, you may also get grants and bonds from the Government of Canada to help with your long-term savings.

Opening a plan will not impact most other benefits

Having a plan does not affect your eligibility for federal benefits.

In most provinces and territories, opening and contributing to a plan will not affect your other benefits, such as monthly disability income support payments or monthly pension payments. In some provinces, making withdrawals from a plan may impact the amount of provincial benefits you receive.

Sections

How much you could get in grants and bonds
Family income and amount of contributions determines how much matching grants and bonds you could get.
Who can open a plan and apply
A plan can be opened at financial institutions for beneficiaries with a long-term and severe disability. You apply for grants and bonds when you open a plan.
Make contributions and watch savings grow
Making contributions to your plan to ensure growth. See how much you could save over time.
If you lose Disability Tax Credit approval
What you can and can't do in an RDSP when the beneficiary has lost DTC approval.
Withdraw money from your plan
Withdraw amounts from the plan at least 10 years after the last time you received a grant or a bond.
Transfer or close a plan
Plans can be transferred from one institution to another. Closing a plan may involve repayments. When a beneficiary dies, there may be a repayment of grant and bond. Any money remaining in the plan after the repayment will go their estate.

If you have questions about the RDSP or Canada Disability Savings Grant or Bond, please contact us.
