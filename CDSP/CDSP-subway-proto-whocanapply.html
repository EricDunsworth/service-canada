Who can open a plan and apply for grants and bonds

The plan beneficiary gets the money; the holder opens the plan

The plan beneficiary is the person with a long-term and severe disability who will receive the money in the future. The plan holder is the one who opens and manages the plan. The beneficiary and holder can be the same or different people.

Both the plan holder and the beneficiary must have a Social Insurance Number (SIN).

Beneficiaries age 18 and under

The plan holder can be a parent, legal representative or public department.

Adult beneficiaries who can open a plan themselves

If an adult beneficiary can enter into a contract and they do not need a legal representative, they must be the holder of their plan.

The beneficiary's financial organization will help determine if the beneficiary is can open a plan themselves.

Adult beneficiaries who can’t open a plan by themselves

If an adult beneficiary cannot open an RDSP due to concerns about their ability to enter into a contract and they do not have a legal representative, certain family members can become the plan holder and open a plan on their behalf.

A qualifying family member could be the beneficiary’s:

  • spouse
  • common-law partner
  • parent

A beneficiary can only hold one plan. Each plan only has one beneficiary.

If you are between the ages of 49 and 59

After December 31 of the year you turn 49, until the age 59, you will not receive the grant or bond from the government. You may still want to open a plan to benefit from contributions being tax-sheltered. That means when you withdraw money from your plan, only the interest earned from your contributions will be taxed by the government. Your original contributions won't be taxed when you make withdrawals. In addition, contributions and money held in your plan do not impact your eligibility for most provincial or territorial benefits.

Apply for grants and bonds when opening a plan

When you open a plan, you can apply for grants and bonds. The amount of grant or bond you receive depends on your family income.

You can receive grants and bonds until December 31 of the year you turn 49.

Open a plan and apply for grants and bonds with a financial organization

Call the financial organization of your choice to book an appointment to discuss opening a plan. You can only open a plan with one of the following financial organizations:

  • ATB Securities Inc.
  • Bank of Montreal
  • Bank of Nova Scotia
  • Central 1 Credit Union
  • Central 1 Trust Company
  • Community Trust Company
  • Concentra Bank
  • Credential Asset Management Inc.
  • Credential Qtrade Securities Inc.
  • CIBC
  • Desjardins
  • Investors Group Trust Co. Ltd.
  • Fonds d'investissements FMOQ inc. (French only)
  • Mackenzie Financial Corporation
  • RBC Royal Bank
  • TD Waterhouse Canada Inc.
  • Natcan Trust Company
